Quantum Coherence in Complex Systems

The Quantum Coherence in Complex Systems section delves deeper into the quantum mechanical aspects that ECM posits as foundational to consciousness. It explicates key quantum concepts:

Wave-Particle Duality

Explains how particles exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties, enabling coherence across different scales.

Superposition

Describes the ability of particles to exist in multiple states simultaneously, contributing to the probabilistic nature of consciousness.

Entanglement

Details how particles become interlinked, with the state of one influencing another instantaneously, regardless of distance.

The section emphasizes the potential role of quantum coherence in facilitating synchronized interactions within neural networks, suggesting a pathway for unified conscious experience. It also addresses the challenges of maintaining quantum coherence in biological systems, such as decoherence, and discusses possible mechanisms (e.g., microtubules) that could protect against rapid decoherence, thereby sustaining coherent states necessary for consciousness.
